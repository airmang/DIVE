# DIVE — 50 Real-User E2E Journeys (design→done)

Each journey = a real user going from goal → PRD design → plan → implement → verify → done inside DIVE. Used to drive gap analysis ([e2e-gap-backlog.md](e2e-gap-backlog.md)) and the live-run subset.


## Static web pages (beginner)

### STATIC-01 — Build a single-page online resume (index.html) with her name, a short bio, education, 3 projects, and contact links — clean and printable, no backend.
*Mina, a 2nd-year university student (non-CS) who needs an online resume to attach to internship applications. Has never written HTML; comfortable with Notion and Google Docs. Korean speaker, runs the app in Korean.*  ·  skill: beginner

- **Design:** Mina creates a new project pointing at an empty folder (~/resume-site), starts a session, and types a plain-language goal: '내 이력서를 한 페이지 웹사이트로 만들고 싶어요'. She expects the app to just start building. Instead DIVE opens a Socratic PRD interview that asks her criterion-linked questions: who's the audience (recruiters), what sections must appear, is print/PDF a requirement, any color/brand preference, what's explicitly out of scope (no animations, no dark mode toggle). She answers in the chat; the PrdAuthoringBoard fills in goal / scope / non-goals / acceptance-criteria (e.g. AC: 'sections render in order Bio→Education→Projects→Contact', 'page prints to one A4 page'). She clicks Confirm PRD and sees the FinalPrdReadView v1; chat ungates.
- **Plan:** DIVE drafts a roadmap from the PRD: step-1 scaffold index.html + basic CSS, step-2 add content sections from her answers, step-3 style for print + responsive, step-4 add contact links. She sees dependencies (2 depends on 1) and short rationales. She doesn't fully understand 'parallel groups' but the step titles are readable. She reviews and clicks Accept; the right-rail PROJECT PLAN appears and run mode moves to Build.
- **Implement:** She runs step-1. The agent proposes a write to index.html; a PermissionCard (warn tier) shows the full diff/patch preview before anything is written. She reads the green/red lines, sees it's just an HTML skeleton, and clicks '이 변경 허용'. read_file calls auto-approve silently. She repeats approve-with-diff per step. After content is written she opens the slide-in Preview tab — because it's a static project, DIVE opens index.html directly (static_file kind, no dev server) and her resume renders in the iframe.
- **Verify:** When the agent claims a step done, a review/provocation card surfaces a criterion-linked question like 'You said it must print to one page — have you confirmed the print layout, or is that still AI self-report?'. The 4-stage evidence-gated stepper (코드 이해 → 점검·관찰 → 검토 응답 → 결정) won't let her hit 결정 until she's actually looked: she opens the Code diff (flips 코드 이해 → Diff 확인됨) and the Preview, then records that the page looks right. She uses Cmd+P in a browser later to truly check print, records the observation, and only then marks the step verified.
- **Done:** All four steps verified; the roadmap shows complete. She has a working index.html she can open in any browser or host on GitHub Pages. A checkpoint exists from before the print-CSS step, so when one restyle made the projects overflow she used the recovery panel to undo back to the last good checkpoint and retried.
- **Good experience:** The PRD interview turns a vague 'make my resume' into concrete acceptance criteria she actually agrees with, so the result matches what she pictured. Diff-preview approvals teach her what each change does without overwhelming her. The static preview opens her real page instantly with zero server/terminal knowledge. The verify stepper makes her glance at the actual rendered page before trusting 'done', and she catches a real print-overflow issue herself — the supervision paid off. Undo via checkpoint removes the fear of breaking it.
- **Watch for:** LLM replies in Korean is fine here (she wants Korean), but the 'print to one A4 page' acceptance criterion is hard for DIVE to verify inside the app — the iframe preview is screen layout, not print layout, so the verify card may push 'evidence' she can only get in a real browser's print dialog; if the stepper treats opening the iframe as sufficient evidence, the supervision is hollow for that specific AC. Also: beginners may rubber-stamp the diff approval without reading it (automation bias) — the card must make the resume content changes legible, not just colored lines. Risk that the PRD interview feels like too many questions before she sees anything visual and she bails.

### STATIC-02 — A single-page portfolio: hero with his name + tagline, a 6-image gallery grid, an about blurb, and an email contact button — responsive, looks good on phone and desktop.
*David, a freelance illustrator in the US who wants a one-page portfolio/landing page to send to clients. Runs DIVE in English. Knows enough to recognize HTML tags but has never built a site from scratch.*  ·  skill: intermediate

- **Design:** David sets locale to English, creates a project at ~/portfolio, and states his goal in English. The Socratic interview asks about the gallery (how many images, where they live, captions?), responsiveness target, and whether he wants a contact form (out of scope — just a mailto button). He notes his 6 images are already in an /images folder. The board captures non-goals (no CMS, no animations) and ACs like 'gallery is a responsive grid: 3 cols desktop, 1 col mobile' and 'contact button opens email client'. He confirms the PRD.
- **Plan:** Plan: step-1 scaffold + hero, step-2 responsive gallery grid referencing /images, step-3 about + mailto contact, step-4 mobile breakpoints polish. He accepts. He expects clear steps and gets them, though he wonders whether the agent knows his image filenames.
- **Implement:** Step-1/2 run; on the gallery step the agent proposes reading the /images directory (auto-approved read) to get real filenames, then a write to index.html wiring the <img> tags. The PermissionCard diff shows the actual img src paths — David checks they match his files before approving. He opens the Preview (static_file) and sees the grid with his real images load from disk.
- **Verify:** Agent claims the responsive grid is done. The provocation card asks a criterion-linked question: 'AC says 1 column on mobile — did you observe it at a narrow width, or is that assumed?'. The verify stepper gates 결정/Decision on evidence. David opens Preview, but the in-app iframe is a fixed width, so to honor the mobile AC he opens index.html in a real browser and resizes / uses devtools, records that observation, then decides verified. He also confirms the mailto button opens his mail client.
- **Done:** Roadmap complete; he has a portable index.html + images he can drag onto Netlify. He made a scope-split mid-way (decided captions were a later nice-to-have) using the plan-adjust flow rather than cramming it in, and a checkpoint let him revert a gradient experiment that looked bad.
- **Good experience:** DIVE reads his actual /images folder so the gallery wires real files, not placeholders — the diff approval is meaningful because he's verifying real paths. English locale stays English throughout. The verify stepper nudges him to actually check mobile rather than trust the AI's 'responsive ✓', and he catches that the iframe can't prove mobile, so he does the real check. Scope-split keeps the plan honest instead of bloating step-2.
- **Watch for:** DEF-01 i18n regression bites this English user hard: secondary status badges and supply-chain tags (실행 중 / 계획 검토 필요 / 검증 필요 / AI 자가보고만 있음) render in Korean even with locale=English, which is confusing and undermines trust for a non-Korean speaker. Also the LLM tends to reply in Korean even at locale=English — for David that's a real defect, not a preference. The mobile-responsive AC is again something the fixed-width iframe can't truly verify, so the supervision risks accepting screen-width-only evidence. If images are large, no risk to the static preview, but the agent guessing filenames instead of reading the dir would produce broken <img> tags.

The verify stepper's 'evidence' for content correctness is just 'did he read it' — easy to click through without truly checking. If any required field (address, phone) is hardcoded-Korean-vs-English non-issue here, but a missing tel: link formatting (010 vs +82) could silently break click-to-call and the app can't detect that. He may abandon if the PRD interview asks anything abstract (audience, scope) instead of just the concrete facts he has in his head.

- **Plan:** Plan: step-1 HTML structure (card + avatar + headline), step-2 the 5 link buttons, step-3 CSS styling + hover, step-4 mobile centering. She reviews dependencies and uses the step list as a mental model of how a page comes together. Accept.
- **Implement:** She runs steps one at a time on purpose, reading every PermissionCard diff to learn the HTML/CSS being added — for her the diff preview IS the lesson. She sometimes uses '요청 수정 / modify request' to tweak the agent's plan (e.g. asks for a CSS class name change) and approves with modified args. She opens the Code tab to study the file and the Preview (static_file) to see each change render.

Checkpoints double as a safe sandbox for experimentation. Running steps one at a time gives her a clean mental model of page assembly.

The verify stepper could feel like friction if she already understands the change; the supervision needs to stay lightweight when she's clearly engaged (over-gating a motivated learner is its own failure). Risk that 'modify request' edits the agent args in a way she doesn't fully grasp, producing a confusing diff. If she expects deep per-line explanations and only gets a one-line summary, the 'learning' value is shallower than promised.
