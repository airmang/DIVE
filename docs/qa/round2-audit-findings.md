## Beginner-readiness rubric (14 dimensions)

Distilled from `.specify/memory/constitution.md` + `docs/research/{vibe-coding-supervision-thesis,conference-poster/01-교수법,internal-research-notes,ai-era-programming-education-direction}.md` + `docs/{student-quickstart,teacher-manual}.md`.

- **[D01] First-run comprehension without help (무지원 첫 진입 이해)** — GOOD: On first launch a true-novice Korean student, with no teacher/manual, can tell within the first screen what DIVE is for (supervising AI to build a real thing) and what to touch first. Onboarding -> provider connect -> project/session -> first card follows the student-quickstart 1→5분 path with each step's success signal visible in-app (e.g. green dot ●, '카드 추가' active only in D). The student is never staring at a blank state with no next action.  
  FAIL: The student opens the app and does not know what it is, what to click, or whether the previous step worked. Required setup (provider key, folder path) appears with no plain-language framing and stalls them. They need the teacher just to get past the first screen.  
  _src: docs/student-quickstart.md (1분~5분 단계, 초록 점 성공신호); 내부 연구 노트 (초심자가 완성품까지 못 가는 문제)_
- **[D02] Plain-language vocabulary load (어휘 부담 / 전문용어 평이화)** — GOOD: Terms a novice cannot be assumed to know — agent, terminal, diff, verify/검증, checkpoint/체크포인트, permission/권한, provider — are introduced in plain Korean with a concrete in-context gloss the first time they appear (e.g. diff = '빨강 지울 줄 / 초록 추가할 줄'). The student can act on a screen without already knowing English jargon.  
  FAIL: Screens assume the student already knows what a diff, runtime, agent, or API key is. Untranslated or unexplained English/jargon ('Execute', 'Provider', 'Verify') sits on primary controls with no gloss, raising limited-English barriers.  
  _src: Target-user definition (limited English, no mental model of agents/terminals/diffs); teacher-manual §2.2 & §3.4 (diff 빨강/초록 평이 설명); ai-era-programming-education-direction §4 (읽기 독해 바닥)_
- **[D03] Criterion & spec scaffolding before instructing (명세/완료조건 비계 — C1)** — GOOD: Before the AI executes, the app scaffolds the student into stating what they actually want with enough precision to later judge it — concrete instruction with input/output/완료조건/금지조건/test examples, not '그냥 알아서 해줘'. The I-step instruction field and AI-interview prompt nudge specificity (e.g. 'placeholder는 example@email.com, 빨간 테두리 X' style), giving the student a checkable criterion they authored.  
  FAIL: The student can fire a one-line vague goal straight into execution with no criterion captured, so there is nothing concrete to verify against later. Specificity is left entirely to the AI; the student never authors a completion condition.  
  _src: conference-poster/01-교수법.md §3 C1 & §7 module1 (SPEC 카드: 입출력·완료조건·금지조건·테스트예시); docs/student-quickstart.md 5분 I단계_
- **[D04] Evidence-before-trust: 'AI said done' != verified (검증 정직성 — C3)** — GOOD: The app visibly distinguishes AI self-report from external/verified evidence. When there is no real test, it must NOT display '✓ 검증 완료' off the agent's own intent_match; verification surfaces ground in concrete project state (diff, output, preview, test result) per Constitution II. The student learns that an AI claim of success is not the same as a verified result, and is prompted to look at the artifact, not the AI's word.  
  FAIL: The UI shows '검증 완료 / ✓' purely from AI self-scoring (intent_match) with test_result=skipped, teaching the student that 'AI said it's done' equals verified. Self-report and external evidence look identical on screen. (This is the exact automation-bias failure the thesis flags.)  
  _src: .specify/memory/constitution.md II (Evidence Before Intervention; AI self-report MUST NOT be treated as verification); vibe-coding-supervision-thesis §3 & §5.2; 01-교수법.md §5 원리2 & §9 검증 엄밀성_
- **[D05] Resisting automation bias / blind approval (반사 승인 방지 — 고무도장)** — GOOD: Approval is a judgment, not a reflex. At minimum the student must open/see what they are approving (diff/output) and register a lightweight call ('확인함 / 우려 있음', or one line of what the change did) before progress — proportionate friction only where evidence warrants it (Constitution V). A student cannot complete the artifact by pressing '승인' N times without ever engaging their evaluation.  
  FAIL: The whole flow can be completed by clicking '승인/수락/모두 수락' repeatedly without ever opening a diff or registering any judgment. The frictionless one-click path lets a novice rubber-stamp; the system never once asks for the student's evaluation. Click is treated as evidence.  
  _src: vibe-coding-supervision-thesis §3 (원클릭 승인) & §6 (맹목 승인 anti-metric); 01-교수법.md §5 원리1; constitution.md V & II (클릭≠증거, 비차단 카드는 sparse)_
- **[D06] Plan critique before execution (계획 비평 한 번 — C2)** — GOOD: Before committing to the AI's plan/cards, the student is prompted to critique rather than just accept — e.g. 'is a step missing? any risk? right scope?' — and can reject/steer. The student authors at least a light critique (빠진 단계 ≥1 또는 우려), practicing steering instead of accepting the first proposal uncritically.  
  FAIL: The AI's proposed cards/plan are accepted wholesale with one button and no prompt to find gaps or risks. The student never practices saying no or adjusting direction; first proposal = final plan.  
  _src: conference-poster/01-교수법.md §3 C2, §4 (계획 비평·승인 판단), §7 module2 (계획비평 카드: 빠진 단계≥1, 위험≥1); vibe-coding-supervision-thesis §5.4 (계획을 비평시키기)_
- **[D07] Error recovery without panic (복구·되돌리기 — C4)** — GOOD: When the AI does something wrong or a file breaks, the student has an obvious, low-fear path: cancel/retry, re-instruct more specifically, or restore a checkpoint — and the app reassures that mistakes are reversible ('실수해도 체크포인트로 복원 가능하니 겁먹지 마'), including that the restore itself is undoable. A novice can get unstuck without the teacher and without re-doing everything.  
  FAIL: When something goes wrong the student has no visible recovery affordance, panics, or restarts from scratch. Checkpoints/restore are hidden or unexplained; the only learned response is '안 돼요' repeated. Restoring feels destructive/irreversible.  
  _src: docs/student-quickstart.md 안전 규칙 & 팁 (Ctrl+S, 초록 점, 다시 시도, 겁먹지 마); teacher-manual §3.1~§3.3 (다시 해줘/Cancel, 체크포인트 복원); 01-교수법.md §3 C4_
- **[D08] Permission / risk legibility (권한 카드 Safe·Warn·Danger 이해)** — GOOD: When the AI wants to touch files or run commands, the student sees a permission surface they can actually read: Safe (auto), Warn (file write -> read diff first), Danger (blocked/explicit read) color-coded and explained in plain Korean, so a novice grasps the risk and that DIVE blocks the truly dangerous things. The student understands they are the gatekeeper.  
  FAIL: Permission prompts are opaque ('Allow tool execution?') with no risk tier, no diff, and no plain explanation, so the novice approves everything blindly or freezes. The student cannot tell a harmless read from a destructive command.  
  _src: docs/student-quickstart.md 안전 규칙 (Safe/Warn/Danger 자동차단); teacher-manual §3.4 (테두리 색·diff 읽기); constitution.md V (blocking gate는 명시적 project-state 사유)_
- **[D09] Trust calibration / suspicion muscle (신뢰 보정 — C5)** — GOOD: The app intentionally exercises the student's 'when to doubt' metacognition — e.g. surfacing 'where might the AI be wrong here?' grounded in the real artifact (Constitution II), so the novice learns plausible-but-wrong outputs happen and that calibrated suspicion (not blind trust nor total distrust) is the skill. Doubt is connected to actually checking.  
  FAIL: Nothing ever prompts the student to anticipate or check where the AI could be confidently wrong; the app implicitly models the AI as always right. The novice leaves either over-trusting (rubber-stamp) or with no way to locate AI error.  
  _src: conference-poster/01-교수법.md §3 C5 & §5 원리4; vibe-coding-supervision-thesis §5.3 (신뢰 보정 훈련 — Bainbridge 정면 대응)_
- **[D10] Real-workflow integrity — no classroom theater (실제 프로젝트 작업만)** — GOOD: Every surface the student touches operates on their real project (Decompose→Instruct→Execute→Verify→Extend) and produces real artifacts. Supervision prompts/cards appear near the relevant artifact as short contextual actions, never as quizzes, flashcards, badges, scores, generic warning banners, or forced long reflections on low-risk steps. The student experiences supervising real work, not a simulation.  
  FAIL: The app drifts into quiz/flashcard/badge/score/standalone-card-deck mechanics, generic AI warning banners with no evidence, or forced reflection gates on ordinary approvals — classroom theater bolted onto coding rather than supervision of real work.  
  _src: .specify/memory/constitution.md I (Real Project Workflow Only), II (review cards near artifact, no quizzes/forced reflection), V; Product Boundaries (no badges/scores/decks/generic banners)_
- **[D11] Cognitive load per screen (한 화면 인지 부하)** — GOOD: Each screen presents one primary task with a clear focal action a novice can find without scanning a dense wall of controls; supervision cards are sparse, deduplicated, and cooldown-aware so the student is not flooded. A true beginner can hold the current step in working memory and is not forced to learn many concepts at once (worked-example→fading respected).  
  FAIL: Screens are crowded with simultaneous panels, jargon, and competing CTAs; review/permission cards fire repeatedly or stack, overwhelming a novice. The student cannot tell the one thing to do now, and cognitive load blocks the actual supervision learning.  
  _src: constitution.md V (cards sparse, deduplicated, cooldown aware; low-friction); 01-교수법.md §2 (worked example/fading, expertise reversal, 초심자 부담↓)_
- **[D12] Knowing what to do next (다음 행동 명료성 / 게이트 = 페이스메이커)** — GOOD: At every point the student knows the current stage and the single next move, and gates read as a pace-setter guiding tempo ('천천히 함께 가라'), not an opaque lock. State signals (stage, '검증 요청'→'승인'→✅ verified, active-only buttons) make the path self-evident so a novice keeps moving without asking the teacher 'what now?'.  
  FAIL: After an action the student is stranded with no indication of what comes next or why a control is disabled; a gate blocks them with no readable reason, so it feels like a punitive lock. The novice repeatedly asks the teacher just to know the next step.  
  _src: 내부 연구 노트 ('게이트는 페이스메이커'); docs/student-quickstart.md 5분 흐름 (검증 요청→승인→verified); constitution.md V (blocking gate needs explicit reason)_
- **[D13] Bilingual parity & locale correctness (한·영 동등성 / 로케일 일관)** — GOOD: The student-facing experience is coherent in Korean: supervisor questions, coach guidance, review cards, buttons, and errors all render in the student's locale (검토 카드 / 확인 필요 카드 wording), with no English text leaking onto Korean screens and no half-translated surfaces. A limited-English novice can complete the full DIVE loop entirely in Korean with equal clarity.  
  FAIL: Korean screens leak raw English (LLM replies in English, untranslated labels/errors), translations are partial or mismatched, or supervisor/coach output ignores locale — forcing a limited-English novice to guess at critical controls or warnings.  
  _src: Target-user definition (limited English); constitution.md II (Korean supervision UI '검토 카드/확인 필요 카드'); S-040 coach write-in-locale & supervisor Korean-question work in repo memory/commits_
- **[D14] Completion = understanding, not just a built artifact (완성≠이해 방지)** — GOOD: Reaching a finished artifact is gated on the student demonstrating they can defend/verify it (e.g. SPEC 대조표 충족/부분/미충족, knowing self-report vs external check), so 'it runs' is not conflated with 'I supervised and understand it'. A novice ends a session able to say what was built, how they verified it, and where the AI might have erred.  
  FAIL: The student finishes with a working-looking output but cannot explain what it does, how it was verified, or where it could be wrong — the app rewarded artifact completion alone and let completion masquerade as understanding (illusion of competence).  
  _src: conference-poster/01-교수법.md §7 module4 (SPEC 대조표 + 이해도 방어 — 완성=이해 혼동을 끊는 장치); ai-era-programming-education-direction §6 (Prather 2024 능력 착각); 내부 연구 노트 (검증할 수 있는 완성품)_

### P1-01 · `chat-cockpit` · error-prevention — Runtime-unavailable state never gates the composer — beginner can send instructions into a dead runtime

- **What**: A provider can be connected (hasConnectedProvider === true) while the supervised runtime itself is unavailable (state==='unavailable', e.g. provider connected but not Pi-capable, missing credentials, or no project root). - **Proposed fix**: Extend deriveInputBlocked to also return a block when runtimeSelection.state === 'unavailable', using the existing concrete runtimeSelection.message/reasonCode as the reason and the existing setupAction (configure_provider / add_credentials / open_project / retry_runtime) as the actionLabel/onAction. This reuses the existing non-blocking inputBlocked banner + action-button mechanism and keeps the message grounded in concrete runtime state (no generic warning banner).
